Lady Jane Grey - The Nine Days' Queen

Lady Jane Grey was an English noblewoman who was declared queen of England on July 10, 1553. Her reign lasted only nine days before she was deposed by Mary I. A highly educated and devout Protestant, Jane was a political pawn used by powerful nobles in a dangerous game of succession. Despite her brief rule, her tragic story and execution at age 16 left a lasting mark on English history.
